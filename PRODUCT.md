# Life2 — Product Context

## Product Purpose
Life2 is a shared PWA for a couple living together. It replaces scattered apps and spreadsheets with one warm, shared space for managing their daily life together: budget, recipes, groceries, and calendar.

## Users
Two people: Nolan and Lylou. Young couple, early-to-mid 20s, tech-comfortable but not tech-obsessed. They use this daily on their phones. The app should feel like something they built for themselves — personal, warm, alive — not a startup product or productivity tool.

## Register
product

## Brand Tone
Warm, joyful, intimate. Feels like a shared notebook, not a dashboard. Playful but not childish. Premium but not cold. The opposite of corporate SaaS.

## Anti-references
- NotebookLM (too neutral, too grey, too "AI tool")
- Notion (too structured, too startup)
- Any generic white + one-accent SaaS UI
- Dark mode productivity tools
- Flat icon grids with identical cards

## Strategic Principles
- This is *their* app. It should feel personal and made-for-them, not off-the-shelf.
- Mobile-first. Most usage is on iPhone, standing in a kitchen or on the couch.
- Joy matters. Small delights, expressive color, something that makes you smile when you open it.
- Shared identity. Two people, two colors (Nolan = yellow, Lylou = pink), one space.

## Color Direction
Warm white base (#FAFAF8). Pastel palette: purple (budget), green (recipes), peach (calendar), blue (groceries), pink (Lylou), yellow (Nolan). Accent purple #7C5CFC. No dark mode. Full palette strategy — color carries personality, not just accent.

## Typography
Outfit (display/headings) + Plus Jakarta Sans (body). Both loaded via Google Fonts.

## Current Stack
Next.js 16, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, shadcn/ui, Supabase
